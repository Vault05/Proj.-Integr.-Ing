Fishing


Disturbed Waters


Most fish are caught in Disturbed Water, patches of the sea where the fish can be seen swimming below the surface. The player can pull up alongside these attractions and attempt to go fishing, but will need the proper equipment. Using the Spyglass ability on disturbed water will allow the player to identify what fish can be caught there; this only works if the player has caught that species of fish before.


There are 8 Fish Types (not including 'fish' caught with Crab Pots)


The player starts with the equipment for catching coastal fish. The remaining types can only be caught using certain rods, nets, or pots, purchasable from the Shipwright and Traveling Merchant after researching.


Fishing in disturbed water involves a mini-game with a fishing meter indicated on the left and a bar that spins around a wheel. As the fish on the meter gets closer to the top, the actual fish is closer to being caught. When the fish icon goes all the way up on the meter, the fish will be caught.


If the player has a functional equipped Rod, then green sections of the bar will appear. The player can click "Fish" or hit the F key while over these green sections to reel the fish in much quicker. Without a rod, it is impossible to catch fish. If all onboard rods are damaged, it is possible to fish in disturbed water, but the green segments will not appear, leaving no choice but to let the meter slowly complete on its own.


Occasionally, a golden segment will appear on the wheel in addition to the green ones. If the player is able to time their click correctly to land on this segment, they will catch a "trophy" fish, with an extra large size and value.


  • There is a 7.5% chance of a trophy notch, or 40% for bait.
  • Five fish must be caught after a trophy for another to have the chance of occurring.
  • Fish caught using bait ignore the five fish cool down between trophy catches and trophy fish can be caught in direct succession at bait locations.

Trawling


Trawling involves using Net. The net can be placed down behind the boat as an Ability and will be dragged along behind it, passively catching fish as the boat moves. The net has its own separate inventory space in which the fish are amassed. Nets will need to be repaired regularly.


There are several fish types that can only be caught through trawling, such as the Aurora Jellyfish and the Anchovy.


Crab Pots


Some creatures can be caught using crab pots. These items can be dropped into the ocean and will passively collect whatever crabs are in the area. If left for too long, however, they will break.


Many creatures can only be caught in crab pots, including the Spider Crab and the Crown of Thorns.


Size


Fish can vary in size. Larger fish are more valuable. A fish that is more than 85% of the maximum size will be considered a "trophy" and be worth a much higher amount than normal. Every fish can be a trophy, including aberrations. At the max size, the base price of a fish is increased by 25%.


Condition


Fish have a condition which slowly degrades. Condition will degrade one level every 12 hours. This condition starts as "Fresh", but slowly deteriorates to "Stale", then "Rotting" and finally deteriorates to become worthless Rottaking up only one inventory slot. Because the status Infected is stored in the same slot as freshness, an Infected Fish cannot progress to a pile of Rot.


  • Fresh fish worth 100% of their value.
  • Stale fish worth 60%.
  • Rotting fish worth 30%.
  • Infected fish worth 30%.

Aberrations


Aberrations are mutated fish that are warped in appearance to their standard counterparts. They can be caught in the same disturbed waters as any other fish. Aberrations are guaranteed when there is a blue glow above the fishing spot. Other ways to increase the odds of catching one include fishing at night, using some cursed equipment, and having a high panic level.
