The Iron Rig

The Iron Rig is a massive industrial installation owned by Ironhaven Corporation, located between Twisted Strand and Devil’s Spine. Built in a remote ocean area, it was officially created to extract resources from the seabed. However, as events unfold, it becomes clear the company is dealing with something far beyond simple extraction.

Unlike the small fishing communities across the archipelago, the Iron Rig represents the arrival of modern industry in the world of DREDGE. Its metallic structure, large machinery, and constant activity contrast sharply with the aged ports and isolated villages elsewhere. Even from a distance, its illuminated silhouette dominates the horizon.

Although the corporation claims its goal is technological and economic progress, numerous incidents suggest otherwise. Disappearances, equipment failures, and impossible phenomena surround the operation. Still, the company continues expanding, ignoring increasingly clear signs that something dangerous lies beneath the depths.

History

When the player first arrives at the Iron Rig, only the base structure is functional. The Supervisor greets the player and asks them to find two Ironhaven Crates to begin basic operations and build the Generator. However, the Generator is not functional yet, as no fuel shipments were sent.

The Supervisor directs the player to the Scientist, who requests another Ironhaven Crate. Once built, the Generator is revealed to require biofuel. After supplying fish, a volcanic-like fissure opens on the seabed, spreading contamination across The Marrows.

The Scientist requests samples from these infected waters using an Infused Rod. Strange ancient species appear, suggesting the fissure is bringing extinct life back. Meanwhile, the situation worsens as contamination spreads further.

The story escalates as executives arrive, drilling intensifies, and the rift expands across multiple regions. Massive tentacles appear, the Leviathan reacts, and corporate decisions push the rig toward collapse. The player is eventually tasked with modifying defenses to stop the operation.

As the experiments continue, the Scientist gradually loses sanity, eventually transforming into an aberration after prolonged exposure to samples from the deep.

Factory

Managed by the Engineer, the Factory requires crates to operate and allows upgrades to the player’s equipment. It has four levels, each unlocked through progression.

  1. Infused Equipment unlocks fishing rods capable of catching altered species.
  2. Material Collection unlocks nets, crab pots, and suction tools.
  3. Repair Technology enables hull repair kits and maintenance tools.
  4. Bait Refinement allows creation of aberrant and exotic bait types.

Technical Laboratory

The Technical Laboratory allows upgrades for lights, fog horns, and hull utilities. It also enables performance improvements for fishing and navigation systems using Ironhaven materials and research parts.

Generator

The first functional building on the rig. Once filled with fish, it powers the entire facility and allows the player to sell resources in exchange for currency, similar to traditional markets.

Science Laboratory

Home of the Scientist, used to analyze samples from affected regions and study biological anomalies emerging from the rift.

Fleet Services

Unlocks ship upgrades up to Industrial Hull Tier 5, allowing major expansion of cargo capacity and durability.

Defenses

Unlocks systems that can alter rig defenses, including devices used to repel large deep-sea entities.

Foundry

Managed by the Smelter, the Foundry allows crafting and refining materials into usable industrial resources.

  1. Material Processing converts scrap into Ironhaven Crates.
  2. Resource Refinement allows conversion between metals, wood, cloth, and dark residue.

Undermarket

A hidden trading system managed by an unknown figure, functioning similarly to traditional shipyard services and handling special resources and explosives.
